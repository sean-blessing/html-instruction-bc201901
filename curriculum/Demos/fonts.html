Fonts

In the examples below, if the font does not look any different than Sans-serif, Serif, Cursive and Monospace samples, then your PC or browser does not support them. You may find that the browser default font matches a PC font or simply fails back a Sarif font. (Sans-serif and Helvetica use Arial on Windows PCs.)

Notes:
  • You should always list multiple fonts that inlcude your preferred list of fallbacks.
       Example: font-family:Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif
  • Fonts with names that include spaces must be enclosed in quotes: 'Arial Black'
  • Font names are not case sensitive.
  • Unknown or misspelled names fall back to serif.
  • Some fonts do not have a bold or italic version. Many browsers will emulate italics and bold.
  • Not all fonts are supported on all operating systems, browsers or devices.
    Codes used in the samples below:
    • Windows - Microsoft font. May be available in non-Windows machines where Office is installed.
    • IE - Typically only displays correctly in Internet Explorer
    • Mac - Apple Macintosh
    • HTML - HTMLdefault font
